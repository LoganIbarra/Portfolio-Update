Completed competitor research and user interviews to better understand how students study vocabulary and what challenges prevented continued learning in the past.

Explored low-fidelity design options with the intent of helping students meet their study goals.

Define

I selected three vocabulary study apps to complete a competitive analysis and learn what types of features were offered and areas for opportunity.

I then conducted three user interviews with individuals who had attempted to use apps to learn vocabulary in the past. This provided insight into why they struggled previously what features a user might need to be successful.

With this information, I formed a proto-persona, Allison. Allison is learning Spanish in her free time after work and she needs to make her study time count. It’s important for her to see progress so she stays motivated.

Allison needs a way to review and be tested on previously learned vocabulary because this keeps her engaged and ensures she recalls the material outside her short-term memory.

We will know this to be true when we see Allison’s repeated use of the app and when she achieves her daily goal for vocabulary words.

I considered some key tasks Allison would need to be successful and established some initial user flows using Lucid Chart. The primary task would be for her to complete her lessons to reach her study goal.

Wireframes

With this data, I sketched some low-fidelity wireframes. I wanted Allison to be able to log in, sign up, study vocabulary through a quiz-type method, and add words to a flashcard deck.

User Testing

Using Marvel, I created a prototype with the wireframes and completed usability testing. I set up a test plan with four direct tasks and corresponding scenario tasks for participants.

There were two key observations from usability testing:

Duplicate content

Proceeding as a guest forced the user to confirm their selection twice

Unexpected navigation

Participants expected to find completed items in the bottom navigation

Iterate

With the information from user testing, I made revisions to my wireframes and updated my prototype.

Reflect

This project reinforced the basics of performing user interviews and usability testing.

I found myself tunneling into small details while creating the paper wireframes and have moved to Procreate to alleviate this. I discovered I work faster in an all digital space and have developed a workflow around this.

Lastly, this solidified my enjoyment of evaluating data and feedback from usability tests.
