A travel app that provides custom itineraries from local guides or travel agents.

Travelers submit their preferences and are provided with itineraries and prices from available partners.

Define

I formulated a list of possible problems the app could solve for our travelers and how it could be useful.

I hypothesized that our travelers…

  • Dollar SignFind a travel agent expensive
  • CalendarDon’t want to adhere to a tour schedule for the whole trip
  • Puzzle PieceHave unique interests that aren’t catered to with other itineraries
  • PencilDon’t feel confident in their ability to research and plan
  • SlidersDesire customization

I crafted my initial problem statement.

Our traveler needs a way to connect to a local travel agent or guide that can offer them customized itineraries because the current market offerings are too structured or expensive.

We will know this to be true when we see trips successfully planned and increased engagement on the app.

With this information, I conducted three in-person user interviews with participants that were in the app’s age demographic, interested in travel, and mobile app friendly.

Takeaways from the user interviews:

Activity-Focused

Participants were active and activity-focused in their previous travels or future interests

Willing to Use Travel Planners

Unlike the majority of survey participants, they had considered or used travel planners

Limited Travel App Knowledge

Participants were unaware of existing apps that offered requested functionalities

I hypothesized that survey participants are traveling to locations they are familiar with, such as visiting family, or prefer more downtime during their vacations. I choose to cater the app towards an activity-focused traveler based on my user interviews.

With my interview notes and audio, I created an affinity map. This affinity map was organized into sections based on participants’ comments. I evaluated this affinity map and made mental notes for things I would improve on next time to make it digestible at the glance.

The affinity map provided further insight into our travelers and how our app can cater to them.

  • StarUse reviews to help make choices
  • HouseAirbnbs are their lodging preference
  • TrainArranging transportation is stressful
  • GemWilling to purchase all-inclusive packages

After completing my research analysis it was time to craft two user personas and user journeys.

Meet Karly, our adventurous traveler. She is a savvy, experienced traveler interested in customization for her entire upcoming trip to the Azores with her spouse. Karly wants to experience new activities, must-see locations, all while getting the best price. She is patient and will wait to secure a deal.

Also meet Shawn, our casual traveler. He often travels with friends and isn’t the main planner. Shawn and his partner are looking to reserve transportation and a lunch recommendation while in Porto. He enjoys researching food and nightlife, then sharing with the group.

I completed a content audit for Mr. Hudson, focusing on their trip design product. You can view the content audit here. This helped me understand how a similar platform was structuring its content and I enjoyed the organizational aspect of the task.

I established initial user flows and a site map to visualize necessary functionalities. Before proceeding with these, I performed a card sort using Optimal Sort with 9 users with my menu naming conventions. I wanted to test how intuitive the navigation was. The card sort simplified my names and structure and was a rapid, inexpensive way to do so.

Wireframes

With my research in hand, I began with some low-fidelity wireframes. I also created wireframes for larger device types to visualize how the elements would scale if necessary.

I evaluated the low-fidelity wireframes and swapped out the panel navigation for a bottom navigation bar to improve usability and remove unnecessary links. I adjusted how a first-time user would create an account. My goal was to reduce duplicate content and streamline access.

With these changes, I created mid-fidelity wireframes in Figma for the sign-in pages, trip planning process, and the “My Trips” pages. These wireframes were used to create a prototype for usability tests.
